1. Platform Overview & Definitions

Key Definitions
Carbon Credit: One tonne of CO2 equivalent reduced/sequestered
MRV System: Monitoring, Reporting, and Verification
Blue Carbon: Coastal ecosystem carbon sequestration
NCCR: National Carbon Credit Registry

The BlueCarbon India platform ("Platform") is a blockchain-powered carbon credit registry and trading platform operated under the Ministry of Earth Sciences (MoES). This agreement governs your access and use of the Platform.

Important: By accessing or using the Platform, you agree to be bound by these Terms and all applicable laws and regulations.

2. User Registration & Account Management

2.1 Eligibility Criteria

To use the Platform, you must qualify under one of the following categories:

3. Carbon Credit Framework

3.1 Credit Generation & Verification

Carbon credits are generated through verified ecological restoration projects following IPCC methodologies:

  • Additionally: Projects must demonstrate carbon sequestration beyond business-as-usual
  • Permanence: Minimum 30-year commitment to maintained sequestration
  • Leakage Prevention: Measures to prevent displacement of emissions

3.2 MRV Process

The Monitoring, Reporting, and Verification process includes:

Phase Description Frequency
Monitoring Satellite imagery, drone surveys, field measurements Quarterly
Reporting Digital data submission through platform Monthly
Verification Third-party audit and validation Annual

3.3 Credit Tokenization

Verified carbon credits are tokenized as:

  • Non-fungible tokens (NFTs) on blockchain
  • Each token represents 1 tCO2e with unique identifier
  • Immutable ownership and transaction records
  • Transparent retirement and tracking mechanism
